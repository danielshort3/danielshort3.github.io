Chrome extension privacy notice

Job Application Copilot Privacy Policy

The extension is designed to keep application evidence and model work on your device unless you explicitly transfer a limited package to the account-backed Job Application Tracker.

Effective and last updated: July 20, 2026

01

Scope

This policy covers the Job Application Copilot Chrome extension, including its side panel, encrypted evidence vault, application-page analysis, local generation, controlled filling, and optional handoff to the Job Application Tracker.

Visits to this website are governed by the site's general privacy policy. The extension itself does not include advertising analytics or behavioral telemetry. If you open this policy on the website, the site's consent-controlled analytics rules may apply to that website visit.

02

Data the extension handles

Depending on the features you use, the extension can handle the following categories on your device:

Verified facts and evidence
Facts you confirm, built-in Website and profile links, custom links, custom thoughts or preferences (including compensation preferences), notes you enter, imported resumes, cover letters, job descriptions, and the original bytes of documents you choose to add.
Derived local records
Extracted text, chunks, embeddings, citations, retrieval indexes, source status, application packets, approved-answer snapshots, clarification records, and integrity metadata used to ground proposals and preserve your work across application pages.
Application context
Supported field structure and bounded job metadata from the active page, such as field labels, question text, options, nearby context, job title, company, location, and a visible job-description section when available. If you choose Use selected page text, the extension also reads only the text you explicitly selected.
Generated work
Proposed answers, source citations, confidence and risk labels, generation caches, and your model preferences.

03

First-use consent and control

Chrome shows the extension's requested permissions at installation. Separately, a blocking first-use screen presents the current versioned data-use disclosure. You must explicitly acknowledge that version before vault creation or unlock, Ollama requests, page analysis or filling, and tracker handoff handlers become available. If the disclosure version changes, acknowledgement is required again.

After acknowledgement, you choose whether to create or unlock the encrypted vault. Imported sources remain disabled for an application until you explicitly select them. The extension scans an application page only after you select Analyze page or Capture current page; selecting page text is a separate explicit action. Filling requires additional review, and consequential fields require review confirmation. Sending a package to the Job Application Tracker is a separate, optional flow with its own review and confirmation steps.

04

Encrypted local vault and browser storage

Evidence records, imported document bytes, extracted content, embeddings, citations, reviewed facts, custom links and thoughts, compensation preferences, generation caches, and application packets containing captured job context, approved-answer snapshots, and clarifications are stored locally in the browser's IndexedDB and encrypted with AES-GCM. The vault key is derived from the passphrase you provide. The passphrase itself is not stored.

What remains outside the vault

The derived key can be retained temporarily in chrome.storage.session so the unlocked extension can operate during the browser session. It is cleared when you lock or reset the vault and when the relevant browser session ends. Non-sensitive preferences, such as selected model names, may be stored in chrome.storage.local; plaintext documents and generation caches are not stored there.

05

Local Ollama processing

Generation and embeddings are sent only to the Ollama service at http://127.0.0.1:11434 on the same device. Setup requires OLLAMA_NO_CLOUD=1. Job Application Copilot rejects cloud models and remote model targets; it does not provide a cloud-model option or send model prompts to a remote Ollama host.

Requests can include the application question, bounded page context, verified facts and links, relevant custom thoughts (including compensation preferences), and excerpts retrieved from sources you enabled. Custom thoughts are displayed as citations when used. Ollama is separate software under your control; its configuration, model files, logging, and local retention behavior are governed by your Ollama setup.

Optional U.S. work-authorization and current/future sponsorship Yes/No selections are encrypted in the vault but are deliberately excluded from retrieval, embeddings, and every Ollama prompt. They are used only through deterministic mappings for clearly U.S.-scoped eligibility questions and are never transferred to the tracker.

06

Application-page analysis and controlled filling

After you invoke analysis or capture, the extension uses temporary access to the active tab to identify supported field structure and job context. It can read labels, field types, select options, validation hints, nearby question context, bounded job metadata, and a visible job-description section when available. Use selected page text reads only text you explicitly select. It does not read or upload the values already entered in form fields.

Hidden fields, CAPTCHA, consent and terms controls, signatures, demographic and voluntary self-identification fields, security codes, file inputs, and submission controls are excluded from actionable Copilot cards. CAPTCHA detection pauses page mutation. When you approve a supported field, the extension applies only that reviewed value through the page's native input behavior. It never clicks submit, accepts terms, signs, consents, or sends an application.

Citizenship, nationality, immigration or visa status/type/class, permanent-resident or green-card status, I-9/E-Verify, export-control, government identifiers, and protected demographic self-identification stay manual and out of model context. Non-U.S. or country-ambiguous eligibility wording is not auto-answered.

07

Optional Job Application Tracker transfer

You may choose to open the account-backed Job Application Tracker with a short-lived transfer capability. The tracker requires sign-in and shows a manifest for review before it requests any selected document bytes.

The transfer is limited to an allowlist of job metadata plus, when you explicitly select them, no more than one resume and one cover letter in PDF or DOCX format, each no larger than 10 MiB. It excludes research, application packets, application questions, proposed or final answers, approved-answer snapshots, clarifications, custom links, custom thoughts, compensation preferences, notes, unrelated vault records, and documents you did not select.

No tracker record is stored until you select Save application. After that confirmation, the transferred material is stored by the tracker's AWS-backed account service and is no longer local-only. Deleting or resetting the extension vault does not delete a saved tracker copy. Delete that copy through the tracker or request help through the contact options below.

08

Why Chrome permissions are requested

activeTab
Provides temporary access to the tab only after you invoke the extension, so it can analyze the current application page.
scripting
Runs the narrow page-analysis and controlled-fill routines in that active tab.
storage
Stores non-sensitive model preferences plus restricted local and session routing or temporary derived-key state. Encrypted vault records are stored in IndexedDB and do not rely on this permission.
sidePanel
Displays the review interface beside the application page.
Loopback host access
Allows requests to http://127.0.0.1:11434 for local Ollama generation and embeddings.
Tracker content script
Runs only on the configured Job Application Tracker URL to complete an optional, user-confirmed handoff.

09

Limited Use and data-use commitments

Use of information received from Chrome APIs complies with the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy, including its Limited Use requirements. Extension data is used only to provide and improve the user-facing Job Application Copilot features you request.

  • Extension data is not sold or transferred for advertising.
  • Extension data is not used for personalized ads, credit decisions, or unrelated user profiles.
  • There is no routine human review of your vault, application context, prompts, or generated answers.
  • Custom thoughts and financial preferences are never publicly disclosed by the extension or included in a tracker handoff.
  • Human review occurs only if you voluntarily send a support message or sanitized diagnostic fixture, and is limited to resolving that request.

10

Retention, deletion, and reset

Encrypted local records remain on the device until you delete individual sources or facts, reset the vault, uninstall the extension, or clear the browser's extension storage. Locking the vault clears access to the derived key but does not delete the encrypted records.

Vault reset permanently deletes the extension's local evidence, application packets, clarifications, approved-answer snapshots, and generated caches from that browser profile and cannot be undone. Cache entries are scoped and invalidated when their supporting content changes. Saved Job Application Tracker records have separate account retention and deletion controls; they are not removed by a local vault reset.

11

Security responsibilities and limits

Local encryption, loopback-only model access, scoped retrieval, and confirmation gates reduce exposure, but no browser extension or local computer is risk-free. Protect your browser profile and operating-system account, use a strong vault passphrase, keep Chrome and Ollama updated, and lock the vault when the device is shared.

You are responsible for reviewing generated content and for complying with the employer's and application platform's rules. Do not use the extension to bypass CAPTCHA, conceal automation, or provide false information.

12

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated when the extension's data practices or features change. Material changes will be reflected by a new effective date on this page and, when appropriate, an in-extension notice or renewed consent step.

13

Contact and privacy requests

For privacy questions, tracker-deletion help, or security reports, use the contact page or email daniel@danielshort.me. Do not include resumes, completed application values, vault exports, or other sensitive records unless specifically requested through a secure channel.
