# Site Content Style Guide

Use this guide for all new pages, tools, and major content updates.

## Title Format
- Use `Page Name | Daniel Short` in `<title>`, `og:title`, and `twitter:title`.
- Keep page names short and task-focused.

## Hero Copy
- `hero-eyebrow`: short context label (3-10 words).
- `h1`: clear primary intent (what the page is for).
- Supporting text: 1-2 concise sentences focused on user outcome.

## CTA Language
- Prefer direct verbs: `Open`, `Compare`, `Download`, `Send`, `Start`, `View`.
- Avoid vague labels like `Click Here` or `Learn More` when a specific action exists.

## Section Recipe
- Use this structure for major sections:
  - `<section class="surface-band ...">`
  - `<div class="wrapper">`
  - `<h2 class="section-title">...` for the section heading
  - `<p class="section-subtitle">...` for the one-line purpose statement
- Keep section subtitle to one sentence where possible.

## Voice And Tone
- Use plain, concrete language.
- Prefer short sentences and active voice.
- Avoid filler, hype language, and repeated claims.

## Punctuation And Symbols
- Use normal apostrophes and quotation marks (avoid mojibake characters).
- Use ampersand (`&amp;`) only where visually appropriate in headings/labels.
- Keep punctuation style consistent within a page.

## Accessibility Copy Rules
- Keep headings descriptive and unique per page.
- Write link text that explains destination/action without surrounding context.
- Keep helper/error text specific and actionable.
